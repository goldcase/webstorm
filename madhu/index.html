Madhu Viswanathan

DIANE AND STEVEN N. MILLER PROFESSOR IN BUSINESS AT THE UNIVERSITY OF ILLINOIS, URBANA-CHAMPAIGN

Madhu Viswanathan has been on the faculty of the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign since 1990, and teaches courses on research methods and on subsistence and sustainability. His research programs are in two areas: measurement and research methodology, and literacy, poverty, and subsistence marketplace behaviors. Madhu has authored books in both: Measurement Error and Research Design (Sage, 2005), and Enabling Consumer and Entrepreneurial Literacy in Subsistence Marketplaces (Springer, 2008, in alliance with UNESCO) .

He directs the Subsistence Marketplaces Initiative and has created unique synergies between research, teaching, and social initiatives. Additionally, he founded and directs the Marketplace Literacy Project, a non-profit organization, pioneering the design and delivery of marketplace literacy education to low-income consumers and subsistence marketplaces.

He has received research, teaching, curriculum development, social entrepreneurship, humanitarian, leadership, public engagement, international achievement, and career achievement awards and his course on subsistence marketplaces was ranked one of the top entrepreneurship courses by Inc. magazine.

He earned a B. Tech in Mechanical Engineering (Indian Institute of Technology, Madras, India, 1985), and a PhD in Marketing (University of Minnesota, 1990).
